Keeping your roof clean is about more than just looks — it’s about protecting one of the most important parts of your home. What is Soft Washing?

Soft washing is a low-pressure cleaning method that uses specially formulated solutions to gently clean your roof. Unlike traditional pressure washing, which can strip shingles and shorten the life of your roof, soft washing carefully removes dirt and biological growth while preserving your roofing materials.

The solution we use is designed to kill algae, moss, and lichen at the root, preventing them from coming back quickly. This method is gentle enough for shingles, tile, and metal roofs, making it safe for most types of residential roofing.

Benefits of Soft Washing Your Roof

High-pressure cleaning can cause shingle damage, leaks, or even gutter problems. Soft washing avoids these risks, keeping your roof intact and protecting your investment.

Algae, moss, and lichen don’t just look bad — they can trap moisture and eventually damage your roof. Soft washing eliminates these growths, keeping your roof healthy and visually appealing.

Regular soft washing prevents the buildup of harmful elements, helping your shingles last longer and reducing the chance of costly repairs down the road.

A clean roof dramatically improves the appearance of your home. Removing black streaks and green patches gives your house a fresh, well-maintained look that stands out in the neighborhood.

Algae and moss can trap moisture and heat on your roof, which may make your home warmer during summer months. A clean roof reflects sunlight better and can even help with energy savings over time.

Soft Wash vs Pressure Wash: Why It Matters

Many homeowners think all roof cleaning methods are the same, but there’s a big difference. Pressure washing is aggressive and can strip away protective granules on shingles, leaving them vulnerable. Soft washing uses gentle pressure with cleaning solutions that do the hard work without causing damage, making it the recommended choice for most roofs.

How Often Should You Soft Wash Your Roof?

The frequency depends on your roof type and local environment. In Brevard County, with our humid climate, algae and moss can build up more quickly. Most homeowners benefit from a soft wash every 2–3 years to keep roofs looking clean and protected. If your roof has heavy tree cover or shows early signs of staining, an annual check and cleaning may be ideal.
