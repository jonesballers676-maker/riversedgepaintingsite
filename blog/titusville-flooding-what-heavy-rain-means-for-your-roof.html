Titusville Flooding: What Heavy Rain Means for Your Roof

Published Oct 28, 2025 • 2 min read

← Back to Blog

Staying Strong After the Storm

Titusville saw record-breaking rainfall and flooding this week — with streets submerged and neighborhoods dealing with standing water. Our team at Robert Jones Roofing hopes everyone in our community is staying safe and dry.

While many homeowners are focused on cleanup from flooding, one area that often gets overlooked after heavy rain is the roof . Even a few hours of intense rainfall can expose weak spots and lead to leaks, hidden moisture damage, or gutter overflow that threatens your home’s structure.

How Heavy Rain and Flooding Affect Roofs

When we experience this kind of rainfall in Brevard County, several roofing issues can appear almost immediately:

• Leaks or water stains in ceilings or attics

• Loose or missing shingles caused by strong winds or pooled water

• Overflowing gutters that push water under shingles or siding

• Flashing damage around vents, skylights, or chimneys

• Hidden moisture buildup , which can lead to mold or wood rot if not addressed quickly

Even if you don’t notice a visible leak yet, small cracks or gaps can allow water to seep in over time — turning into costly repairs later.

What Titusville Homeowners Should Check Right Now

After the flooding, take a few minutes to visually inspect your roof and surrounding areas:

✅ Look for missing or lifted shingles from the ground.

✅ Check your gutters and downspouts for debris buildup.

✅ Inspect your attic or ceiling for damp spots or musty smells.

✅ Make sure water is draining away from your home’s foundation.

✅ Keep an eye out for pooling water near roof valleys or low spots.

If you’re unsure about what you’re seeing — or you simply want peace of mind — it’s best to have a professional take a closer look.
