How Much Should You Pay a Roofing Contractor?

Published Jan 29, 2025 • 3 min read

← Back to Blog

Introduction

When it comes to home improvements, roofing is one of the most critical investments you can make. Whether you're repairing a minor leak or replacing an entire roof, hiring the best roofing contractors ensures the job is done right. But one of the biggest concerns homeowners face is cost—how much should you pay a roofing contractor? Prices can vary significantly based on several factors, including materials, labor, location, and the complexity of the job. In this guide, we’ll break down the costs associated with roofing projects so you can make an informed decision and avoid overpaying.

Factors That Affect Roofing Costs with Roofing Contractors

The cost of hiring a roofing contractor depends on multiple factors. Understanding these variables will help you determine a fair price for your roofing project.

1. Type of Roofing Material

The materials you choose play a significant role in determining the overall cost. Some of the most common roofing materials and their estimated costs per square foot include:

• Asphalt shingles – $3 to $7

• Metal roofing – $5 to $14

• Tile roofing – $7 to $15

• Slate roofing – $10 to $30

High-end materials like slate and tile are more durable but come with a higher price tag. On the other hand, asphalt shingles are more affordable and widely used by homeowners. The best roofing contractors will help you choose the right material based on your budget and climate.

2. Labor Costs and Contractor Experience

Labor costs can vary depending on the expertise and reputation of the contractor. Experienced roofing contractors typically charge more, but their quality of work justifies the higher price. On average, labor costs range from $1.50 to $3.00 per square foot .

If a contractor offers a significantly lower price than competitors, be cautious. Extremely low bids can indicate a lack of experience, low-quality materials, or even a potential scam. Always ask for references, check reviews, and ensure the contractor is licensed and insured before hiring.

3. Roof Size and Complexity

The size and design of your roof also affect the final cost. Roofing contractors typically measure jobs in "squares," with one square equaling 100 square feet . A larger roof means more materials and labor, increasing the cost.

Complex roof designs with multiple slopes, chimneys, skylights, or steep angles require more expertise and additional safety precautions, leading to higher costs. The best roofing contractors will assess your roof and provide a detailed estimate based on its complexity.

4. Additional Repairs and Upgrades

If your roof has underlying issues, such as rotting wood, damaged insulation, or poor ventilation, these problems must be addressed before the new roof is installed. Additional repairs can increase costs but are essential for long-term durability. Some common add-ons include:

• Roof decking replacement – $2 to $5 per square foot

• Gutter installation – $3 to $8 per linear foot

• Flashing replacement – $200 to $500

The best roofing contractors will inspect your roof thoroughly before starting the project, ensuring all necessary repairs are factored into the estimate.

How to Get the Best Price on Roofing Services

While roofing is a significant investment, there are ways to ensure you're getting the best value for your money.

1. Get Multiple Quotes

Always obtain at least three quotes from different roofing contractors. This allows you to compare prices, services, and warranties. Be wary of extremely low or high estimates—an unusually low bid could indicate subpar materials or workmanship, while an overpriced bid may be unnecessary.

2. Ask About Warranties and Insurance

A reputable roofing contractor will offer both manufacturer warranties (for materials) and workmanship warranties (for installation). Make sure the contractor is licensed and carries liability insurance to protect you from potential issues during the project. The best roofing contractors will always provide documentation and be transparent about their policies.

3. Consider Seasonal Discounts

Roofing companies tend to be busiest in late spring and summer. If possible, schedule your project in the off-season (late fall or winter) when contractors are less busy and may offer discounts.

4. Avoid Storm Chasers and Scams

After major storms, some contractors go door-to-door offering quick, cheap roof repairs. These "storm chasers" often provide low-quality work and disappear once the job is done. Always research the contractor before agreeing to any work.

Conclusion

The cost of hiring a roofing contractor depends on various factors, including materials, labor, roof size, and additional repairs. While it may be tempting to go for the lowest bid, investing in a reputable and experienced contractor ensures quality workmanship that will protect your home for years.
