Why Brevard County Homeowners are Switching to Metal Roofing in 2026

Published Mar 12, 2026 • 3 min read

← Back to Blog

As we settle into March, the Florida sun is already starting to dial up the heat. For homeowners in Titusville , Melbourne , and across the Space Coast , this time of year usually brings two things to mind: rising electric bills and the realization that hurricane season is just a few months away.

In 2026, we’ve seen a massive shift in how our neighbors are protecting their homes. While asphalt shingles have been the "standard" for decades, metal roofing has officially become the gold standard for Florida living. Curious about the switch? Get a 30-second metal roofing quote here.

At Robert Jones Roofing , we’ve seen first-hand why local families are making the switch. Here is why a metal roof is the smartest investment you can make for your home this year.

1. Beating the "Space Coast Heat"

Florida’s UV exposure is among the highest in the country, and standard dark shingles act like a sponge for that heat, cooking your attic and forcing your AC to work overtime.

Modern metal roofing systems use advanced reflective coatings (Cool Roof technology) that can reflect up to 85% of solar radiation. In fact, many of our customers see a 20–30% reduction in their cooling costs almost immediately. In a climate where the AC runs ten months out of the year, those savings add up fast. See how much you could save on a more energy-efficient roof with a free estimate.

2. 2026 Insurance & The "15-Year Rule"

The Florida insurance market has changed significantly over the last year. Many carriers are now scrutinizing roofs once they hit the 15-year mark—and some are even refusing to renew policies for shingle roofs of that age.

The Metal Advantage: Insurance companies love metal. Because a standing seam metal roof is far more resistant to wind uplift and fire, many providers offer premium discounts for homeowners who upgrade. While a shingle roof might struggle to get insured after 15 years, a well-maintained metal roof is often insurable well into its third or fourth decade. Don’t let your insurance drop you—request a free roof inspection today.

3. Built for the Storms of Tomorrow

The 2026 Florida Building Code is stricter than ever, especially for coastal areas like Titusville and Merritt Island. Metal roofs are engineered to withstand winds of 140–150 mph —ratings that traditional shingles simply can't match.

By choosing a standing seam profile, you’re getting a system with concealed fasteners. This means there are no exposed screws for the salt air to corrode or the wind to get under, making it the most hurricane-resistant option on the market today.

4. The "Last Roof" Investment

It’s no secret that a metal roof costs more upfront than shingles. However, the math tells a different story over time:

• Asphalt Shingles: Usually last 12–18 years in Florida before the sun and storms take their toll.

• Metal Roofing: Designed to last 50 to 70+ years .
