Local vs. Storm Chasers: How to Find a Reputable Roofer for Your Home by Robert Jones Roofing

Published Sep 24, 2025 • 4 min read

← Back to Blog

The unexpected sound of hail hitting your roof, the drip-drip-drip of a leak during a heavy rain, or even just the tell-tale signs of aging shingles – these are moments that send many homeowners into a frantic search for a roofing contractor. But in the aftermath of a storm, or even just when seeking routine maintenance, a different kind of storm can brew: the onslaught of "storm chasers." These transient companies often swoop into affected areas, make big promises, and then disappear just as quickly, leaving a trail of shoddy work and unresolved issues. How do you distinguish between these fly-by-night operations and a reputable, local company that stands by its work? The key lies in knowing what to look for and understanding the value a trusted partner like Robert Jones Roofing brings to your home.

The Dangers of Dealing with Storm Chasers

Storm chasers typically follow severe weather patterns, moving from one disaster zone to the next. Their business model relies on quick repairs, often at inflated prices, before they vanish. This can leave homeowners in a precarious position. Imagine discovering a problem with your roof months after a repair, only to find the company's phone number disconnected or their office address leads to an empty lot. Warranties become worthless, and you're left to foot the bill for another repair. These companies often pressure homeowners into signing contracts immediately, sometimes even offering to cover your deductible, which is a red flag for insurance fraud. They may use inferior materials, cut corners on installation, or even cause more damage than they fix. Choosing a local, established company helps you avoid these costly and stressful scenarios, ensuring accountability and peace of mind.

Why Local Expertise Matters Most - Robert Jones Roofing

A local roofing company, like Robert Jones Roofing , has a vested interest in the community it serves. Their reputation is built on word-of-mouth referrals, local reviews, and long-term relationships with homeowners. They live and work where you do, which means they are familiar with local building codes, common weather patterns, and the specific challenges your roof might face. This intimate knowledge ensures that the solutions they provide are not only effective but also durable and compliant with all regulations. Furthermore, a local company is accessible. If you have a question a year down the line or need an additional service, they're just a phone call away, with a physical office you can visit. This enduring presence fosters trust and provides a reliable point of contact for all your roofing needs. They're not just looking for a quick buck; they're building a business that thrives on customer satisfaction.

Essential Qualities of a Reputable Roofer

When you're sifting through potential contractors, look for these crucial indicators of a reputable roofing company:

• Proper Licensing and Insurance: This is non-negotiable. Ask for proof of both. A legitimate roofer will have general liability insurance to protect your property and workers' compensation insurance to protect their employees in case of an accident.

• Local References and Reviews: A strong online presence with positive reviews on platforms like Google, Yelp, and the Better Business Bureau is a good sign. Even better, ask for local references you can contact directly to hear about their experience.

• Clear, Detailed Estimates: A trustworthy roofer will provide a comprehensive, written estimate that breaks down all costs, materials, and the scope of work. Be wary of vague quotes or pressure to sign immediately.

• Manufacturer Certifications: Reputable roofers often hold certifications from roofing material manufacturers (e.g., GAF Master Elite, CertainTeed SELECT ShingleMaster). These certifications mean they've undergone specific training and can offer enhanced warranties.

• Physical Business Location: A local office that you can visit is a strong indicator of a legitimate business, unlike storm chasers who often operate out of temporary lodging. When you work with Robert Jones Roofing , you're choosing a company with deep roots and a strong commitment to the local area.

Asking the Right Questions

Don't be afraid to ask potential roofers a series of direct questions. This not only helps you gather information but also gives you a sense of their professionalism and transparency.

• "Can you provide proof of your licensing and insurance?"

• "What kind of warranty do you offer on your workmanship, in addition to manufacturer warranties?"

• "How long have you been in business in this local area?"

• "Can you provide a list of local references?"

• "What is your process for handling unexpected issues or changes during the project?"

• "How do you ensure proper cleanup after the job is complete?"

These questions will help you vet contractors and identify those who are truly committed to quality and customer service. Choosing a roofer is a significant decision for your home, and an informed choice can save you from future headaches and expenses.

Conclusion

When it comes to your home's most vital protection, settling for less is never an option. While the allure of a quick fix from a storm chaser might seem tempting, the long-term risks far outweigh any short-term benefits. By prioritizing local expertise, checking for proper credentials, and asking the right questions, you can confidently choose a reputable roofing partner who will protect your investment for years to come.
